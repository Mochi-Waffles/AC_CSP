BASKING SHARKS (Cetorhinus maximus)

Basking sharks may look terrifying to some, but to others, they're a wonder. These sharks are found in the Atlantic and pacific oceans, staying in warmer climates. They usually stay near the surface, though during migration they can dive up to 4,147 feet underwater. Dispite their large size of up to 10 meters or 36 feet, Basking sharks stick with a special diet of zooplanktin, barnacle larvae, and fish eggs. The way they eat, is they slowly float around in the ocean and wait for their food to drift in their mouths. Basking sharks are the second largest fish in the world that are alive today. They have a lifespan of 30 years, though it's not official yet, it's speculated they can live longer.

GOBLIN SHARKS (Mitsukurina owstoni)

If you had a hard time loving the basking shark, the goblin shark is even more interesting. The goblin shark isn't their only name, sometimes they are known as the elfin shark. Goblin sharks feed on many different types of fish, some being the dragonfish and rattails, which are very facinating creatures on their own. Their jaws can extend from their mouth with a snap, making it easier for them to grab their prey. Their jaws can snap nearly 10 times faster than any other sharks can. Thya can also extend their jaws far enough to be the same length as their nose. Goblin sharks can grow 20 feet long, living for something in between 20 to 60 yars, which is a large gap. Many different sources say different things about how long they usually live for, it can change along iwth their living conditions. If one was held in captivity, we would expect to see it live for around 60 years, though in the wild it's 25, which is why there's such a large contrast.
